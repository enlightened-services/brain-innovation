The Science Behind TM

Over 380 peer-reviewed studies demonstrate the profound effects of Transcendental Meditation on brain function, stress reduction, and overall well-being.

Enhanced Brain Coherence

EEG studies show increased alpha wave coherence across brain regions during TM practice, indicating improved communication between different parts of the brain.

Stress Reduction

Research demonstrates a 30% reduction in cortisol levels, the body's primary stress hormone, with regular TM practice.

Improved Performance

Studies show enhanced creativity, better decision-making, and improved cognitive function in TM practitioners.

Cardiovascular Benefits

Meta-analysis reveals 48% lower risk of heart attack or stroke in regular meditators compared to control groups.

Better Sleep Quality

EEG data shows normalization of sleep patterns and increased deep sleep phases in TM practitioners.

Reduced Anxiety

Clinical trials demonstrate significant reduction in anxiety symptoms, with effects lasting beyond meditation sessions.

Understanding Brain Waves

Different mental states produce distinct electrical patterns in the brain. Here's what the Muse headband measures:

Delta
0.5-4 Hz

Deep sleep and healing. Rarely seen during waking meditation.

Theta
4-8 Hz

Deep relaxation, creativity, and intuition. Present during deep TM states.

Alpha
8-13 Hz

Relaxed awareness and calm focus. Dominant during TM practice.

Beta
13-30 Hz

Active thinking and concentration. Decreases during meditation.

Gamma
30-100 Hz

Peak cognitive performance. May increase in experienced meditators.
